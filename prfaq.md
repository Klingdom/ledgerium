# PRFAQ — Ledgerium AI

## Press Release

### Ledgerium AI Introduces the Universal Work Ledger  
**A Truth-First Foundation for Process Intelligence and Safe Automation**

**Boise, Idaho — [Future Date]**

Ledgerium AI today announced the Universal Work Ledger, a new category of infrastructure designed to make organizational work observable, auditable, and trustworthy before it is optimized, automated, or augmented by artificial intelligence.

Most organizations rely on interviews, dashboards, and inference to understand how work happens. Ledgerium replaces narrative with immutable evidence.

At its core, Ledgerium captures structured signals of real work as it occurs and preserves them as append-only ledgers. From this evidence, workflows, standard operating procedures, and process intelligence are deterministically derived and always traceable back to source truth.

Ledgerium is not a monitoring tool, not a workflow dashboard, and not an autonomous AI agent. It is foundational infrastructure for process understanding, designed with privacy by architecture, determinism, and auditability as non-negotiable constraints.

Unlike traditional process mining and task capture tools, Ledgerium never records screenshots, keystrokes, content, or credentials. All capture is local-first, inspectable as plain JSON, and explicitly controlled by the user.

Ledgerium includes an optional AI layer that explains results but cannot invent structure, override evidence, or hide uncertainty. If sufficient evidence does not exist, the system explicitly says so.

Ledgerium is currently available through an early access program for builders, operators, and organizations preparing for AI and automation responsibly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. What problem does Ledgerium solve?

Organizations do not actually know how work happens.

Most process understanding is based on interviews, workshops, dashboards, and inference. As a result, automation encodes assumptions, AI hallucinates, and improvements fail to compound.

Ledgerium solves this by creating a durable system of record for work itself.

---

### 2. What is the Universal Work Ledger?

The Universal Work Ledger is an append-only, immutable record of structured work evidence.

It captures what happened, in what order, and with what structure — without capturing sensitive content. All workflows, metrics, and intelligence in Ledgerium are derived from this ledger.

---

### 3. How is Ledgerium different from process mining?

Traditional process mining infers workflows from incomplete system logs.

Ledgerium:
- Captures work at the interaction level
- Preserves raw evidence immutably
- Derives structure deterministically
- Allows humans to explicitly anchor meaning
- Prevents silent reinterpretation

Ledgerium treats process understanding as an evidence problem, not a modeling problem.

---

### 4. Is Ledgerium employee monitoring software?

No.

Ledgerium does not monitor people. It records structural signals of work.

It does not capture screenshots, video, keystrokes, content, messages, or credentials. It does not score individuals or rank productivity.

Ledgerium exists to understand process, not to surveil workers.

---

### 5. How does Ledgerium protect privacy?

Privacy is enforced by architecture, not policy.

- Capture is local-first
- Users control when data is exported or uploaded
- Raw data is inspectable as plain JSON
- No covert capture is possible
- Access and retention are explicitly governed

This design aligns with enterprise privacy, security, and compliance requirements by default.

---

### 6. What does “deterministic” mean in Ledgerium?

Determinism means the same input always produces the same output.

Derived artifacts — steps, activities, process maps, and metrics — can always be rebuilt from raw evidence. There are no hidden heuristics or probabilistic reinterpretations.

This guarantees auditability and trust.

---

### 7. What is the Session Recorder?

The Session Recorder is a lightweight, local capture tool.

It records:
- Structural interaction events
- Navigation and focus changes
- Idle and boundary transitions
- Explicit human markers for decisions and exceptions

It never records content.

---

### 8. What role do human markers play?

Human markers allow users to explicitly annotate decisions, exceptions, and boundaries.

These markers override inference and ensure derived structure reflects reality rather than assumption. Humans anchor meaning in the system.

---

### 9. What is the Raw Data Schema?

Ledgerium enforces a strict, versioned JSON schema.

The schema acts as a contract across capture, ingestion, derivation, and interpretation. Invalid or ambiguous data is rejected before entering the system.

This prevents silent drift and ensures long-term stability.

---

### 10. How does Ledgerium generate process intelligence?

Ledgerium deterministically derives:
- Steps
- Activities
- Process maps
- Temporal boundaries
- Decision points

From these, it computes metrics such as time distributions, variance, decision density, and rework — all grounded in evidence.

---

### 11. Does Ledgerium use AI?

Yes — optionally and in a constrained role.

AI in Ledgerium:
- Explains results
- Answers questions bounded by evidence
- References derived artifacts directly

AI cannot invent structure, override evidence, or fill gaps with guesses. If evidence is insufficient, Ledgerium says so explicitly.

---

### 12. Can Ledgerium be used without AI?

Yes.

All core functionality works without AI enabled. AI is an interface layer, not a dependency.

---

### 13. Why not blockchain?

Ledgerium does not rely on blockchain.

It achieves immutability and auditability through append-only storage, cryptographic hashing, deterministic pipelines, and rebuildable artifacts — without the complexity, privacy risk, or operational cost of blockchain.

Optional external attestation may be offered in the future but is not required for trust.

---

### 14. Who is Ledgerium for?

Ledgerium is for:
- Operators and builders
- Process and operations leaders
- IT and platform teams
- Compliance and risk organizations
- Enterprises preparing for AI responsibly

It is not designed for surveillance or productivity scoring.

---

### 15. How does Ledgerium scale?

Ledgerium scales by preserving immutable evidence and deriving all intelligence deterministically.

This prevents compounding error as automation increases and ensures trust remains intact as systems grow.

---

### 16. What does success look like for Ledgerium?

Success means:
- Organizations trust their process understanding
- Improvements compound over time
- Automation is safe and explainable
- AI remains grounded in reality

Ledgerium succeeds when truth becomes infrastructure.
