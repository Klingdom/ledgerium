Anticipated Questions & Crisp Answers

How is the Health Score calculated?
"0–100, weighted across four honest dimensions: Speed (healthy time band, 30 pts), Consistency (run-to-run variation, 30 pts), Data Quality (extraction confidence, 20 pts), Standardization (SOP/doc readiness, 20 pts). Transparent — not a vanity metric."
How does it decide "Automate" vs "Standardize"?
"A transparent rule order: high automation-opportunity score + multiple systems + healthy → Automate; high run-to-run variance → Standardize; too slow → Optimize; low trust/health → Monitor; otherwise Healthy. Every tag is auditable."
Why is everything "Healthy" / stability 100%?
(Honest one — turn it into a strength.) "Most of these are single captures, so consistency is at its max by definition. Variance and variant analysis gets genuinely interesting once a process is recorded multiple times — which is exactly when we surface 'standardize' and 'automate' signals."
How is this different from Scribe / Tango?
"They make a how-to guide. We baseline the process, measure it, find variants and bottlenecks across runs, and tell you where AI pays off — with an evidence trail. They document; we generate process intelligence."
How is this different from Celonis / UiPath process mining?
"Mining needs months of clean event-log integrations. We capture real behavior directly from the browser in minutes, and link every insight back to actual evidence. Faster to value — and the recommend→execute loop is the moat."
Is our data safe / where does it run?
Speak to determinism + immutable raw evidence + your security posture. Have a one-liner ready.
