# Ledgerium AI

**Truth-first infrastructure for process intelligence.**

Ledgerium AI builds the **Universal Work Ledger** — foundational infrastructure that makes organizational work observable, auditable, and trustworthy **before** it is optimized, automated, or augmented by AI.

Ledgerium is not a monitoring tool.  
It is not a dashboard.  
It is not an autonomous AI agent.

Ledgerium is **infrastructure for process truth**.

---

## Why Ledgerium Exists

Most organizations do not actually know how work happens.

Process understanding today relies on:
- Interviews and workshops  
- Dashboards and averages  
- Partial system logs  
- Inference-heavy models  

As a result:
- Automation encodes assumptions  
- AI systems hallucinate  
- Compliance is hard to prove  
- Improvements fail to compound  

Ledgerium replaces narrative with **immutable evidence**.

---

## Core Idea

> **Process understanding is an evidence problem, not a modeling problem.**

Ledgerium captures structured signals of real work, preserves them immutably, and deterministically derives workflows, SOPs, and process intelligence that remain traceable to source truth forever.

---

## High-Level Architecture

**Design principles**
- Evidence before intelligence  
- Determinism over inference  
- Privacy by architecture  
- Rebuildability forever  
- AI as interface, not authority  

---

## Core Components

### 1. Session Recorder
- Local-first capture of **structural work signals**
- Explicit human markers for decisions and exceptions
- No screenshots, no keystrokes, no content, no credentials
- Outputs inspectable JSON session ledgers

### 2. Immutable Work Ledger
- Append-only, content-addressed evidence store
- Raw truth preserved independently of interpretation
- All derived artifacts are rebuildable from source evidence

### 3. Schema Contract & Validation
- Strict, versioned JSON schema
- Validation at capture and ingestion
- Prevents silent drift and ambiguity

### 4. Deterministic Derivation Engine
- Transforms evidence into steps, activities, and process maps
- Same input → same output
- No probabilistic inference or hidden heuristics

### 5. Process Intelligence Engine
- Measures **process behavior**, not people
- Time distributions, variance, decision density, rework
- Designed for continuous improvement and AI readiness

### 6. Optional Interpretive AI
- Explains results using derived artifacts
- Cannot invent structure or override evidence
- Explicitly returns “insufficient evidence” when appropriate
- Fully disableable

---

## Privacy by Architecture

Ledgerium enforces privacy by design, not policy.

- Local-first capture  
- Explicit user control over export and upload  
- Raw data always inspectable  
- No covert data collection  
- No individual performance scoring  

Ledgerium exists to understand **process**, not to surveil people.

---

## Process Intelligence Platform

Ledgerium functions as a **Process Intelligence Platform**, enabling organizations to:

- Identify flow breakdowns and instability  
- Reduce rework and hidden delays  
- Prove compliance using real evidence  
- Prepare safely for automation and AI  

Because all intelligence is derived from a single immutable source, improvements **compound over time**.

---

## Video Evidence Expansion

Ledgerium is expanding into video processing by treating video as **another evidence modality**, not surveillance.

- Video is converted into **structured signals** (state changes, motion, boundaries)
- Signals become ledger events
- Raw footage is optional, local-first, and time-limited by default
- No facial recognition, identity tracking, or behavior scoring

Ledgerium evolves toward a **Universal Work Evidence Ledger** while preserving determinism and trust.

---

## Why Not Blockchain?

Ledgerium does not rely on blockchain.

Instead, it uses:
- Append-only immutable storage
- Cryptographic hashing
- Deterministic pipelines
- Rebuildable artifacts

This provides stronger auditability, better privacy, and lower enterprise risk than blockchain-based systems.

Optional external attestation may be supported, but blockchain is **not required for trust**.
